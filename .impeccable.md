## Design Context

### Users
Wedding guests — friends and family of Hazim & Idayu (Malaysian couple). Viewed on mobile phones primarily, before and during the wedding day (June 2026). Guests range from younger attendees comfortable with digital invites to older family members who need clarity above all.

### Brand Personality
**3 words: soft, romantic, botanical**

Intimate and personal — this is a love letter to guests, not a corporate product. The tone is warm, unhurried, and quietly celebratory. It should feel handcrafted, like a beautifully printed card, not designed-by-committee.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Light. Warm cream and white backgrounds. Never dark or moody.
- **Palette**: Sage green (#7a9e87) + warm gold (#c9a84c) on cream (#faf8f5). Tinted neutrals, never pure white or black.
- **Typography**: Great Vibes (script) for names — this is the emotional anchor. Cormorant Garamond (serif) for supporting type. Josefin Sans for labels and microcopy (tracked caps).
- **Visual motif**: Botanical — hand-drawn-feeling leaves, stems, and florals in sage. Present but not overwhelming; they frame the content without competing.
- **Anti-references**: Generic Canva wedding templates, purple/rose-gold gradients, maximalist floral overload, glassmorphism, anything that looks like AI generated it.

### Design Principles
1. **Names first** — Hazim & Idayu are the focal point. Every other element exists to frame them.
2. **Restraint as luxury** — White space and small type signal quality. Don't fill every gap.
3. **Warmth over perfection** — A small personal touch (the parking quote from Hazim) is worth more than pixel-perfect polish.
4. **Mobile-native** — Designed for a phone in one hand. Touch targets, readable text, no horizontal scroll.
5. **Botanicals as soul** — The SVG botanical illustrations are not decoration; they're the visual personality of the design.
